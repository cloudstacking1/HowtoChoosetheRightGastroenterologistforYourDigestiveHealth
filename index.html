What Is Gastroenterology?

Gastroenterology is a medical field focused on the digestive system, including the stomach, intestines, liver, pancreas, and other organs involved in digestion. Gastroenterologists are doctors who specialize in diagnosing and treating diseases and conditions that affect the gastrointestinal tract. This includes everything from common issues like acid reflux to more complex and chronic conditions such as Crohn’s disease, ulcerative colitis, and irritable bowel syndrome (IBS).

What Does a Gastroenterologist Do?

A gastroenterologist is a highly trained medical professional specializing in the diagnosis and treatment of digestive disorders. They have extensive knowledge of diseases that affect the digestive organs and are skilled in performing specialized tests and procedures, such as colonoscopies, endoscopies, and biopsies.

Some of the conditions treated by gastroenterologists include:

  • Acid reflux and GERD (Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease)
  • Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)
  • Inflammatory bowel diseases (IBD) like Crohn’s disease and ulcerative colitis
  • Gastrointestinal bleeding

Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and acid reflux are common conditions where stomach acid irritates the esophagus, causing symptoms like heartburn, regurgitation, and chest pain. If you are struggling with these symptoms, our team can provide you with an accurate diagnosis and recommend the best treatment options to manage and alleviate your discomfort.
